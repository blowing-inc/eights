EIGHTS — USER GUIDE
The game of improbable battles
================================================================================

WHAT IS EIGHTS?

Eights is a tournament tool for friends. You invent combatants — fictional
characters, inside jokes, real people, abstract concepts — draft a roster, and
battle them out round by round with your group voting on each result. The host
makes the final call. Everything that happens is saved permanently: the
combatants, the winners, the reactions, the chat, the story.

The goal is not just to win. It's to end up with a record of something that
happened between real people that you can read back years later and still laugh.


================================================================================
GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

--- Accounts vs. Guests ---

You can play as a guest by just entering a name. Guests work fine for a single
session on one device.

If you create an account (tap the user icon in the top-right corner), your
history, open lobbies, and combatants are tied to that identity. You can pick
up a game from any device by logging in. Logged-in accounts are the preferred
state — particularly for the host.

Guest sessions use browser storage and can be lost on a hard refresh or when
switching between devices.


--- Creating a Room ---

Tap "Create a room" on the home screen. You'll set your name and configure
the room settings before the lobby opens.

ROOM SETTINGS:

  Roster size (3–12, default 8)
    How many combatants each player drafts. This determines the number of
    rounds — one round per combatant slot. A roster of 8 means 8 rounds.

  Allow spectators
    When on, people can join with a spectate link and watch without playing.
    They see rounds, chat, and reactions in real time but can't vote.

  Anonymous combatants
    Hides the owner's name on each combatant card during the voting round.
    Useful if you want voting to be purely on the combatant, not the player.

  Blind voting
    Individual votes are hidden until everyone has picked. Prevents pile-ons.
    The result is revealed all at once when the last vote is cast.

  Bios required
    Forces every player to write a short bio for each combatant before locking
    in their roster. Recommended if your group leans into the narrative.

Once the room is created, share the 4-character code or use the copy-link
buttons to invite players and spectators.


--- Joining a Room ---

Tap "Join a room" and enter the room code. If the game is already in progress,
you may be offered a spectate option instead of joining as a player.

You can also join via a shared link, which will pre-fill the code.


================================================================================
THE DRAFT
================================================================================

Each player sees a private form with numbered slots. Fill in a name for each
combatant. Bios are optional unless the host turned on "Bios required."

Your draft is auto-saved as you type. If you close the tab and return, your
progress will be restored.

Lock in your roster when you're done. Other players' drafts are hidden — you
don't see anyone else's combatants until the first round begins.

--- Heritage Drafts (Series) ---

If this game is part of a series — a continuation of a previous battle — your
champions from the last game are required entries. You'll see your previous
winners listed and must place each one in a slot before you can submit. They
appear highlighted in your draft form.

If a champion evolved since the last battle, their evolved form appears instead
of the original. That variant carries the same owner and the same obligation.

--- Force Start ---

The host sees a readiness tracker once they've submitted their own draft. If
most players are ready, the host can force-start — any player who hasn't
finished their draft won't have their combatants in the game. Use this when
someone has gone quiet or the group is ready to move on.


================================================================================
BATTLE ROUNDS
================================================================================

The battle screen shows the full round list as it fills up. Rounds are started
by the host — tap the round button to advance.

Each round opens a voting screen for all players. One combatant per player is
shown in the matchup. Players tap the combatant they think should win and
submit their vote. The host sees all votes.

The host then:
  · Confirms a winner — locks in the result and moves to the next round, or
  · Declares a draw — both combatants are recorded as draws, no winner.
  · Evolves the winner — see the Evolution section below.

The host can also undo the last round if a mistake was made, as long as the
next round hasn't started.

--- End Game Early ---

The host can end the game before all rounds are played. The game is marked as
ended early. Combatants from that game won't be published to the Bestiary
(since the game was incomplete).

--- Tournament Complete ---

When all rounds are done, the host has three options:
  · View history — see the full record of the game
  · Continue series — start a new draft with the same players (see Series)
  · Complete tournament — marks the game as finished and returns home


================================================================================
EVOLUTION
================================================================================

Evolution is the narrative heart of the game. When the host confirms a winner,
they can choose to evolve that combatant instead — recording how this victory
changed them.

HOW IT WORKS:

1. The host clicks "Evolve ⚡" on the winning combatant.
2. The host decides who writes the evolution — themselves, or they can hand
   it to the combatant's owner.
3. The writer sees a form with the combatant's current name pre-filled. They
   give the evolved form a new name. The bio field starts with the original
   bio as a baseline — they can rewrite it entirely or extend it.
4. On submit, the name is checked for uniqueness across the entire Bestiary.
   Evolution names must be novel — you can't evolve into something that
   already exists.

WHAT EVOLUTION CREATES:

  · A new entry in the global Bestiary — the evolved variant is a distinct
    combatant with its own win/loss record, reactions, and bio.
  · A permanent evolution record on the round — storing who evolved from whom,
    what opponent caused it, and the game it happened in.
  · The original combatant is preserved exactly as it was. Its bio does not
    change. Its stats continue to accumulate in future games.

WHAT EVOLUTION DOES NOT DO:

  · It does not replace the original in any other round of the current game.
    The draft roster is immutable — each combatant fights in its assigned slot.
  · It does not affect the current game's remaining rounds.

IN THE NEXT BATTLE (SERIES):

  When a series continues, the evolved form appears in the owner's draft as a
  required pick — the owner must place the variant in a slot. The original is
  not required in its place; only the most recent evolved form is.

  If a combatant evolved multiple times across multiple games, only the current
  tip form (the most recent variant) appears as the required entry.

READING THE EVOLUTION IN HISTORY:

  Every round that had an evolution shows "⚡ → [evolved name]" in the round
  list. The full round detail shows the evolution callout with the story:
  "[Original] evolved to [Variant] after the fight with [Opponent]."


================================================================================
BATTLE HISTORY
================================================================================

Tap "Battle history" from the home screen to see all recorded games.

Games in a series are grouped together. Each group shows the total rounds
played across the series and the date range.

--- Room Detail ---

Tap a game to open its full record:

  · Winners list — a quick summary of every round result.
  · Round navigator — step through each round to see the combatants, votes,
    reactions, chat, and evolution record.
  · Combatant roster — all combatants from the game, sortable by player.
    Shows each combatant's result (W/L/D) and who they faced.

--- Series Standings ---

For a series (multiple connected games), the expanded series view shows
standings: wins, losses, and total games per player across the whole series.

--- Continue Series ---

If you hosted a completed tournament, you'll see a "Continue series" callout
in the room detail. Tapping it starts a new draft with the same players,
carrying forward prevWinners and evolved forms.

If you need to abandon a series after launching the next draft, you can
"End series" from the draft screen. The completed games are preserved in
history and the series can be resumed later.


================================================================================
THE BESTIARY
================================================================================

The Bestiary is the permanent public record of every combatant ever entered
across all games — once a game completes, its combatants are published here.

Each entry shows:
  · Name and bio
  · Win/loss/draw record
  · Reactions (❤️ 😡 😂) accumulated across all games
  · Owner
  · For variants: the lineage — what they evolved from, who they beat, and
    which game it happened in.

SEARCH:
  Use the search bar to find combatants by name, bio text, or player name.

SORT:
  Sort by wins, losses, reaction counts, or alphabetically.

VIEW MODES:
  · Characters — shows only base (generation-0) combatants. Each character
    appears once. Variants are surfaced inside the combatant's detail page.
  · All forms — shows every published form including evolved variants.

COMBATANT DETAIL:

  Tap any combatant to see their full profile:
    · Current name and bio
    · Stats grid: wins, losses, draws, total battles
    · Head-to-head breakdown (expandable) — who they faced most and their
      record against each opponent
    · Evolution lineage — for variants, the full story of every form they
      came from and what caused each evolution
    · Bio history — previous versions of the bio if it was ever edited
    · Reactions total


================================================================================
SPECTATORS
================================================================================

If the host allowed spectators, a separate share link is available in the
lobby. Spectators join via that link and see:
  · The lobby and player list
  · The current round's combatants and matchup
  · The round results as they are confirmed
  · A running round history

Spectators cannot vote, react, or chat. They observe only.


================================================================================
HOST CONTROLS (SUMMARY)
================================================================================

  Lobby:       Share room code/link, start game, cancel room
  Draft:       See readiness, force-start, cancel tournament / end series
  Vote round:  Confirm winner, declare draw, trigger evolution, undo last round
  Battle:      Start next round, end game early, complete tournament, next battle


================================================================================
KNOWN TICKETS (INCONSISTENCIES NOTED DURING GUIDE WRITING)
================================================================================

TICKET 1 — Spectators can't react or chat
  Spectators see the round but have no way to express themselves. The guide
  currently describes this as intended, but reactions-from-spectators could be
  a natural feature. Worth discussing.

TICKET 2 — "Undo last round" not shown to non-hosts
  Non-host players have no indication that a round can be undone or that it
  was undone. The round list just changes. A small notification on the
  non-host battle screen when an undo happens could improve clarity.

TICKET 3 — Evolution name uniqueness check is published-only
  The uniqueness check (checkCombatantNameExists) only compares against
  published combatants. An unpublished combatant from a currently-running game
  could share a name with an evolution target and pass validation — then both
  get published. Low probability but worth noting.

TICKET 4 — Force-start behavior vs. bios-required setting
  If "bios required" is on and the host force-starts, players who were skipped
  may have combatants without bios published anyway (or not at all, since their
  draft wasn't submitted). The guide says "their combatants won't be published"
  but this may not be the whole story. Worth verifying the interaction.

TICKET 5 — Guest "My open lobbies" persistence
  Guest open lobbies are tracked in localStorage by room ID. If a guest clears
  storage or switches browsers, the lobby tracker is lost. The guide mentions
  this. It may be worth showing a "You may have active games — enter a code to
  rejoin" prompt on the Join screen for guests.

TICKET 6 — Series standings don't account for draws
  computeSeriesStandings counts wins and losses per player across a series but
  the standings table columns are W/L/G. Draws are tracked on combatants but
  the standings table has no D column. If draws matter competitively this is
  a gap; if series standings are purely narrative it may be fine.

TICKET 7 — "Complete tournament" vs. auto-end phase
  When all rounds are played, the room phase stays as 'battle' until the host
  explicitly taps "Complete tournament." Until then, the room can appear in
  open lobbies. The guide describes this flow correctly, but a first-time host
  may not realize they need to tap Complete. A more prominent prompt or
  auto-completion on all-rounds-done could help.

TICKET 8 — No confirmation when host navigates away mid-round
  If a host taps "← Home" during an active voting round, there is no warning
  that players are waiting. The vote screen persists for other players but the
  host has effectively abandoned it. Low severity for in-person play, higher
  for remote.
